The Workplace is a Construct

September 29, 2025

87% of managers report their technical input adds value. 71% of engineers report that same input delays decisions without improving them.

Frames

Erving Goffman showed that humans operate through interpretive frames, and that we cannot perceive the frame we're in. Bolman and Deal identified four of them in organizations:

  • Structural: roles, responsibilities, hierarchies
  • Human Resource: needs, relationships, development
  • Political: power, coalitions, competition
  • Symbolic: culture, meaning, ritual

An engineering manager spends three hours in architecture review. Through the HR frame, she's mentoring and building relationships. The senior engineers are in the political frame, establishing territory, demonstrating influence. The junior engineers are in the structural frame, absorbing patterns, learning craft. The organization is in the symbolic frame, performing culture.

All four frames operating simultaneously. All four correct.

Anthony Giddens called it the gap between practical consciousness (what people do) and discursive consciousness (what people say they do). Frames collide in that gap.

Neural Architecture

Dacher Keltner's research found that power reduces mirror neuron activity by 35-50% within weeks. Ian Robertson showed that promotion triggers measurable brain changes: testosterone up, dopamine circuits rewired, cortisol suppression.

There's a window, usually two to six weeks, where the newly promoted manager oscillates between frames. They can still access their engineering mindset Monday morning, lose it in the partner meeting Tuesday, glimpse it again during code review Wednesday.

"I lost sight of my sense of purpose and my added-value as an engineer in the company."

The neural architecture changes within weeks. Technical skills persist longer, months or years, but become inaccessible through the changed frames.

Mirror neurons enable modeling others' mental states. Without them, managers can't conceptualize autonomous work.

"I am no longer allowed to manage my own time. It is constantly interrupted and work must flow through her."

The promotion creates this transformation. Predictably. Measurably. The construct produces exactly what it's designed to produce.
